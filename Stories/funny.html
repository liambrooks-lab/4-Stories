THE GHOST WHO FAILED JEE

In the crumbling hostel of Kota, where dreams came to die and rank lists were written in tears, lived a boy named Aryan. He gave JEE 2024 with the confidence of a toppper and the preparation of a potato. Result? Rank 6,94,20,069. Even the AI counselling system laughed at him.

That night he hanged himself from the ceiling fan using his Allen module as a noose. The fan, being as overworked as the students, broke immediately. Aryan died, but his ghost stayed behind — extremely pissed off and academically frustrated.

Now every night at 3:33 AM he appears in random students’ rooms across India. He doesn’t scare them with blood or chains. No. He does something far worse.

He sits on their bed and starts explaining Rotational Mechanics in the most boring voice possible while crying about his percentile.

“Bhai… integration toh kar liya tha… phir bhi -4 aaya… tu bata na kya galti thi…”

If you try to sleep, he starts solving previous year questions loudly. If you ignore him, he brings out his own answer sheet and makes you check it. The red marks are still wet with his tears.

Last week he possessed a boy in Delhi who was watching reels at 4 AM. The boy woke up at 6 AM solving organic chemistry named reactions perfectly. His mother thought he had achieved enlightenment. In reality, Aryan had possessed him for 2 hours and forced him to study.

The worst part? He only leaves when you promise to attempt at least one mock test the next day. Many students have started sleeping with their admit cards under the pillow as protection.

Moral of the story: Fail karna bhi ek talent hai…
